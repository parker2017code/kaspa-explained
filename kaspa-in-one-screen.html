The artifact

The short version.

What it is

A mined UTXO network that tries to make Proof-of-Work payments feel closer to real time.

What is live

PoW mining, UTXO model, blockDAG structure, GHOSTDAG ordering, the Crescendo-era 10 BPS network, and ecosystem tooling around KRC20/KRC721 standards.

Why it matters

It tests whether Bitcoin-style mined money can feel closer to real time without becoming a normal centralized fast database.

What is next

Toccata, vProgs, native DeFi, DAGKnight, 100 BPS, TangVM, and RTD-derived oracle or attestation flows sit in targeted, roadmap, or research lanes until activation evidence changes their status.

What would make it work

Durable users, healthy mining, realistic node operation, liquidity, fees, wallets, developer tools, useful post-Toccata apps, and careful roadmap execution.

What would weaken it

Overclaiming roadmap work, weak adoption, poor liquidity, mining or infrastructure concentration, unclear security budget, or app-layer promises outrunning shipped software.

Mental shortcut

The basic frame.

Bitcoin-style mining culture, seconds-scale user feel, blockDAG ordering, ecosystem-token tooling today, and future apps that prove their own rules.

That is the compact public narrative. The important boundary is that KRC standards are ecosystem tooling today, while Toccata, native assets, and vProgs are separate protocol and roadmap lanes.

Productive uses now

Where KRC tooling can be practical.

Coupons and rewards

A shop can issue a KRC721-style coupon or a KRC20 reward credit, then redeem it in its own checkout or membership system.

Events and access

A festival can use tokens, passes, collectibles, or event credits for entry, perks, food, merchandise, or proof-of-attendance workflows.

The boundary

Required deploy/mint gas fees are miner fees, but wallets or frontends may charge separately. Redemption, refunds, metadata, fraud handling, and legal rules remain the issuer's job.
