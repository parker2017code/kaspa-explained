What is live, what is not.

Last verified: May 4, 2026. This is a Kaspa-positive research guide, not neutral financial advice. It explains the protocol argument and reference trail; it does not prove valuation, adoption, liquidity, mining economics, regulatory treatment, or future app demand.

Question Short answer
Is Kaspa live? Yes: Proof of Work, UTXO, blockDAG, and GHOSTDAG are live mainnet features.
Is 10 BPS live? Yes: the Crescendo-era 10 BPS upgrade is live; block rate is not the same as unlimited throughput or instant finality. Use the rusty-kaspa repository, releases, and KIP-14 discussion before public summaries.
Is Toccata live? No. The current public target window is roughly June 5-20, 2026, pending testnet, audit, rehearsal, and final activation work. Some older roadmap pages may still show May 5. Michael Sutton's Toccata outlook is the clearest implementation-context source for that timing.
Are vProgs or native DeFi live? No. They are roadmap/application-architecture directions that depend on shipped foundations and tooling.
Is DAGKnight live? No. DAGKnight belongs in the research and upgrade track, not current mainnet behavior.
What can users do today? Send, receive, self-custody, mine or study mining, run nodes, use wallets, explorers, visualizers, and learn the live 10 BPS blockDAG system.

One visual idea

Bitcoin chooses one path. Kaspa orders parallel work.

A normal blockchain has one selected chain. When honest miners find blocks at nearly the same time, one path wins and the other competing blocks usually become wasted side blocks. Kaspa's blockDAG starts from a different shape: honest parallel blocks can reference one another and GHOSTDAG gives nodes a rule for ordering that graph.
